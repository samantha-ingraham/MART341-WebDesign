Get a library card at MPL

In person

  1. Visit the Missoula Public Library
  2. Bring a photo ID and proof of address
  3. Fill out the application
  4. Receive your library card

Online form instructions

  1. Visit the Missoula Public Library website.
  2. Hover over the "services" tab and click on "Library Cards"
  3. Fill out the online form and submit. This will give you a temporary card number
  4. Visit the library for in person verification. Make sure you have your ID and proof of address.
  5. Receive your library card

Additional Tips and Helpful Info

  • Mountain Line Bus Schedule and Routes
  • People without a permanent place of residence can apply for a limited use library card and check out up to two items until they have settled in Montana.
  • You can sign up for a library card at any Public Library in Montana, and it will work at ALL of them!!
  • If you don't have proof of address, you can send yourself a postcard from the library, and then return with it as your proof of address
  • Missoula Public does not charge late fees! They will only charge you if you never return an item, or if you return it damaged. They will accept good condition copies as replacements.

Signing up for Libby

Visit this video walkthrough if you get lost at any point!

  1. If you intend to use the app, open your phone's app store and download the Libby app
  2. Select "I have a Library Card". Some libraries don't require residency to get a library card. See below for information on signing up for one of them
  3. Click "Search for a library" and then enter your zipcode
  4. If you have a Missoula public library card, click the "MontanaLibrary2Go" option, this is the consolidated online library for the entire state!
  5. You should be taken to the Montana Library page. In the bottom right hand corner, click on "sign in with library card"
  6. Click on your appropriate home library
  7. Enter your library card number and start borrowing!!

Additional Tips and Helpful Info

  • Some libraries don't require you to live nearby to sign up for a library card. Please visit Libraries without in person verification to sign up for one of those! Thank you to Noemi for the up to date list.
  • Libby has a lot of built in features that can help you keep track of things you want to read.
  • Libby will automatically return borrowed items on the due date unless you extend them
  • You can only extend your loan so many times
  • If you put a hold on something, and it becomes available, you'll get notified right away
  • You can also let the next person borrow it if you aren't ready to read your hold yet, you'll get the chance to borrow it again when they're done rather than having to wait in line again
  • Visit this video walkthrough if you get lost when signing up at any point!
