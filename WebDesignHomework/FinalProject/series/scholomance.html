The Scholomance by Naomi Novik

Content Warnings: Mature content/description of sex(not erotica), Violence, Character death, Gore

Magic Boarding School of Nightmares

The Scholomance trilogy is set in an unforgiving world, where survival is the first lesson. Most of the story takes place in a boarding school out of nightmares, The Scholomance, which is the best protection available for young wizards from the Malefecaria that want to eat them while they learn how to survive.

The series opens with A Deadly Education, introducing us to El. A prickly, sarcastic, and immensely powerful protagonist who defies the typical "Chosen One" archetype, El is an outcasted loner who spends most of her time resenting her peers for their unfounded dislike of her. Her narration varies wildly between scathing cynicism, dry humor, rightoues anger, and floundering despair(which tracks, as she is in fact a teenager). El's goals are short list: survive graduation and join an enclave. Those goals start to give way under the pressure to avoid her grandmother's prophesy and her empathy for the people left on the outside of an enclaves safety. As El gains allies, her priorities begin to change, and she puts everything on the line to protect them.

The sequel, The Last Graduate, ups the ante, throwing El and her allies into even greater danger as they prepare for their final year and the deadly gauntlet of graduation. Novik deepens her exploration of the Scholomance's mechanisms, expanding on its lore and the moral dilemmas surrounding the school's "survival of the fittest/wealthiest". El's relationships— particularly with the golden boy Orion Lake —become more nuanced, adding layers to her character development. The slow-burn romance and the tension between self-interest and altruism reach a fever pitch as graduation draws near, setting the stage for the series' explosive conclusion.

The Golden Enclaves takes the story beyond the walls of the Scholomance, unraveling long-kept secrets about the magical world and its dark underpinnings. The series' concluding book reveals many of the secrets present in the preceding two novels, while leaving some questions unanswered. I will not spoil anything, but I will say that I cried more than once over the course of the final book.

What sets Scholomance apart is its subversion of familiar fantasy tropes. The series challenges the notion of heroism and power, offering a critique of privilege, exploitation, and the cost of survival in an unjust system. If you can't see the critique of our current society, then you aren't trying. Novik's writing is razor-sharp, with moments of biting humor and profound poignancy. The pacing is relentless, particularly in the second and third books, where the stakes feel ever more insurmountable.

The Scholomance trilogy is a dark and thought-provoking addition to the fantasy genre. Naomi Novik has crafted a series that doesn't just entertain but also challenges us to think about the nature of power, community, and the choices we make when the opportunity to thrive while others suffer confronts us. For fans of wizarding variety fantasy, this series is an absolute must-read.
